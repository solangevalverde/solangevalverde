CoLab Peru's Website

Objective

I obtained a Co-Op that requested I create a website for a project for SFU Graduate Beedie School called CoLab Peru. Previously they only had a blog where as now they needed a brand new website where they could store information that can be used for conferences and the people involved with them as well as anyone else who wanted to help or stay updated with their work. The website also has information about how to get involved.

Process

To start the website I needed to be sure what they wanted me to create, that required multiple meetings with the team in order to be sure I could cover all their needs. Through this meeting I came to know they were also in need of a graphic designer, so I not only was their web developer, but also became their graphic designer. I started to work on a small branding for them including logos, icons, banners and more. This was done so they could all present said branding during their national and international talks.

One of the problems I encounter was the fact that during my starting days the team kept on travelling, which made meetings to define what the website needed difficult to set. Even though we tried to have online meetings, some of the places they travelled to had weak internet connection. This led to many revisions. Luckily once the setup meetings were set and the information needed was provided, I was able to design and develop a website that not only had the aesthetics they wanted to project, but also that covered all their concerns.

Another problem that came up was that I was the sole web developer, and because my contract was as an intern, they were not going to have a developer to update their website continuously. Furthermore, due to the team’s lack of knowledge on programming, they did not have the skills required to update the website on their own.

This let me to find some plugins as their website was created with WordPress. The plugin that I found allowed them to keep edit the page as they see fit, whenever they wanted, without the need of a developer. This was implemented, as many people on the team had to update their own work to their audience, and having to wait for a developer each time only to update would not be efficient. Using this plugin I was able to design and create the whole website and left some template pages so they could copy and use to keep their website updated.

This project came to be very important to me as I learned on how to work with non-developers, seeing their needs and creating solutions for them. I believe the project was a success as the website is still being used to this day.
