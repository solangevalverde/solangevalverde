Alpha Pi Phi's Website

Objective

I was in charge of all socia media publication and interaction with anyone who would message the sorority's media accounts. This allowed me the chance to create all the graphics and posts in our social media. I was also in charge of the sorority's website, which was previously hosted on Wix. During the first few months, I just edited all the Wix content and the design. After talking with the President of the sorority, we came to the decision that the website needed to be completely redone. The website linked on this page is the fully redone version of the website.

Public Relations Process

For the graphics posted on our social media, I made sure to use for backgrounds either images that were CC0(Creative Commons Zero) or completely my designs. I created Facebook banners, Instagram posts.

Website Process

For the Website, I first recreated it using Wix and keeping slightly the same style and idea that the previous Web Designer had. I felt that it still required quite more work and that it was not as polished as it could be. That's when I decided, inspired on an assignment I did for a Web Design class, to redo the entire site on my own. This website is no longer hosted by Wix.

Before starting to redo the website I had to sit down with the Vice President of the sorority to talk what pages were necessary and crucial to be accessible as fast as possible. We kept the president informed on what we had as maybe she had a different request. I also did research checking other sorority wesites, what they all had in common and how some stood out by using different elements.

Different pages required to talk to different position holders. For example for the President's Message I emailed the President requesting an inspirational message for whoever might be interested in becoming part of the sorority. For Rush events (activities with sisters and women who are interested in joining the sorority) I met with the Membership Developer Officer to see what her vision was and work along schedules and graphics.
